  • Description

  • Little to no additional sanding
    Since sanded plywood is sanded at the mill, little to no additional sanding is required for most applications.

Sanded plywood features face and back (top and bottom) plies that are sanded in the manufacturing process. It is used in places where it is visible, such as for cabinets, shelves and paneling. Hardwood plywood is made from hardwoods like birch, maple and oak.

Uses
Sanded plywood is often used for projects such as cabinets, shelves, paneling, and bookshelves. It can be painted or stained to create a finished look.
Manufacturing
Sanded plywood is made by sanding the top and bottom plies of the plywood during the manufacturing process. It usually consists of three or more panels with cross-laminated layers of wood veneer.
Sanding techniques
When sanding plywood, it's recommended to start with coarse grit and gradually increase to medium and fine grits. You can use a sanding block or pad to distribute pressure evenly and prevent uneven surfaces. It's also important to remove dust between each grit so that the next grit can properly smooth the surface.
Finishing
After sanding, you can apply a finish to protect the plywood and enhance its appearance. The type of finish you use depends on the type of plywood and how you intend to use it.
